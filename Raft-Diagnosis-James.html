Raft — Growth Diagnosis
Prepared for James Coombes, Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer

The Market Is Moving. The Growth Function Is Not.

Raft has the proof. The problem is no one is building the motion to capture what is opening right now.

The Core Leak

In January 2026, CargoWise's first invoices landed. Freight forwarders who had tolerated years of friction saw cost increases of 20 to 50% overnight. The Loadstar called it "a level of agitation rarely seen around a software update." Forwarders who had quietly wondered about alternatives are now actively looking.

The current motion — conferences, tariff content, earned press — built the first 60 customers. That motion is referral-shaped. It works slowly, through relationships, over 6 to 12 month sales cycles. When forwarders type "CargoWise alternative" or flag switching intent across LinkedIn, Slack communities, or industry forums, there is no system at Raft to see it, route it, and act on it.

Posting a fractional Demand Generation Director role on April 2, 2026 confirms Raft knows the gap. But by the time any new resource finds their footing, the CargoWise evaluation window will have closed. The disruption is not waiting. Every week without a structured outbound motion is a week of qualified forwarders landing somewhere else.
What This Seems to Indicate

The content function and the commercial function are not connected.

Raft publishes strong material: tariff survival guides, AI in customs compliance articles, podcast appearances with The Loadstar. But content does not automatically reach the 200 mid-size forwarders who would buy tomorrow if they saw the right case study. There is no account-level distribution layer converting content into qualified conversations.

The proof exists. The activation does not.

Masterpiece International: 93% document automation, 1,000 labour hours saved per month. ALS Customs: 10 times faster customs clearance, 80% error rate reduction. These numbers are real and rare in this category. But they live on a resources page — not being sent directly to specific accounts, timed to specific triggers.

The buyer profile makes this harder than most SaaS markets.

Freight forwarders do not respond to outbound the way SaaS buyers do. They are relationship-first, sceptical of AI claims, and they buy through peer networks. Win rates on new customers in logistics run at 13.4% versus 22.7% for existing accounts. Any new resource without the right infrastructure in place will not build the right motion fast enough.

40% of the top 25 global freight forwarders are already customers.

That means 60% are not. And many of those 60% are now reconsidering their technology stack. The list of forwarders publicly signalling dissatisfaction with CargoWise in trade press and industry forums is not short. None of that signal is being systematically captured and worked right now.

The demand gen signal is telling.

Fractional means lower commitment, faster to value, less risk. That is the right instinct for a team that needs results in 90 days, not 9 months. But without the right account data, sequences, and targeting framework in place, any new resource defaults to spray-and-pray. The infrastructure has to be installed first — or the first 90 days get spent building from scratch instead of generating pipeline.

The CargoWise disruption is a 12 to 18 month window. Forwarders are already in evaluation mode. Some will make decisions in Q2 2026. The ones who engage with a clear, specific, proof-backed case for Raft early will convert. The ones who do not hear from Raft will land with Reform (Y Combinator-backed, building the same document automation play on a narrower scope) or with one of the Descartes ecosystem tools — or they will stay with CargoWise and absorb the cost.

Raft's product is stronger than any of those alternatives at the platform level. But the story is not being told to buyers who are evaluating right now. There is no comparison page on raft.ai. There is no outbound reaching the right accounts with the right message at the right time. There is no warm sequence turning the tariff content into a direct conversation.

What I Would Fix First
1

Build the target account list from live signals.

Start with 20 to 30 mid-size freight forwarders who have publicly signalled CargoWise frustration: in trade press, LinkedIn commentary, or industry forum posts. These are warm accounts. They are not cold. Reaching them now with Raft's proof is a very different conversation than cold outbound. This list does not exist yet. It should exist this week.

Unlocks: A working account list the acquisition function can act on from day one — not month three.
2

Activate the case studies as outbound assets, not search content.

The Masterpiece International and ALS results are conversion-grade proof. They need to be reformatted as one-page, account-specific documents sent directly to comparable firms. A forwarder with 300 staff and 12 offices reads "1,000 labour hours saved per month at a firm your size" differently than they read a blog post. This alone is enough to open conversations.

Unlocks: Existing proof assets doing the job of new sales collateral — without any new content budget.
3

Map the sales cycle to the CargoWise contract calendar.

CargoWise contracts renew annually. Forwarders who received January 2026 invoices are evaluating now. Those renewal dates are knowable. A targeting system built around contract renewal timing turns a 6 to 12 month sales cycle into a 60 to 90 day conversation — and the outbound motion starts producing pipeline from week one.

Unlocks: A timing advantage over every competitor approaching the same forwarders without renewal intelligence.
4

Build one specific outbound sequence now.

A targeted 3-step sequence for forwarders evaluating CargoWise alternatives — using Raft's proof metrics, industry-specific language (pre-alerts, customs entry filing, clearance by exception), and a direct comparison to what the price increase costs annually — turns the CargoWise disruption into active pipeline immediately.

Unlocks: Pipeline conversations starting in weeks, not months.

Right now, Raft's new customers come to them. Conferences, referrals, industry relationships: demand is inbound and unpredictable. The CargoWise disruption creates a short period where outbound can work in this market in a way it almost never does in freight. Forwarders are looking. The question is whether Raft reaches them before the window closes, or catches up after the decisions are made. A structured, signal-led approach to this specific moment would convert the same proof assets Raft already has into predictable new business conversations — installed and producing output in weeks, not months.
